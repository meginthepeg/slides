Open Data

Licensed/ Proprietary Data

Open data is defined by the gvt of canada as structured data that is machine-readable, freely shared to be used and built on without restrictions.
Gold standard, what about best effort? The key things to remember about open data are:
Availability and access: the data must be available as a whole preferably by downloading over the internet. The data must also be available in a convenient and modifiable form.
Re-use and redistribution: the data must be provided under terms that permit re-use and redistribution including the intermixing with other datasets.
Universal participation: everyone must be able to use, re-use and redistribute. .
Not all data can be open data. Sometimes an organization is unable to release data as open data because of certain restrictions.

Proprietary data refers to data that is controlled or owned by an organization. It's protected by copyright, trade secret laws, and even a patent agreement. Accessing this data usually means paying a sum of money and entering into an agreement with the license holder.
